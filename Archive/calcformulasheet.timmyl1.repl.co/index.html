Properties of Limits:

If L, M, c, and k are real numbers and lim f(x) = L and lim g(x) = M

Sum Rule: lim (f(x) + g(x)) = L + M

Difference Rule: lim(f(x) - g(x)) = L - M

Product Rule: lim (f(x) * g(x)) = L * M

Constant Multiple Rule: lim (k * f(x)) = k * L

Quotient Rule: lim f(x)/g(x) = L/M

Power Rule: lim(f(x))r/s = Lr/s

Endpoint:A function y=f(x) is continuous at a left endpoint or right endpoint of its domain if limx->a+ f(x) = f(a) or limx->b- f(x) = f(b), respectively.

If the functions f and g are continuous at x = c, then the following combinations are continuous at x = c:

  2. Differences: f + g
  3. Products: f * g
  4. Constant multiples: k * f; for any number k
  5. Quotients: f/g provided g(c) ≠ 0

Chapter 4

Chain Rule

If ƒ is differentiable at the point u = g(x), and g is differentiable at x, then the composite function (ƒ ∘ g)(x) = ƒ(g(x)) is differentiable at x, and (ƒ ∘ g)′(x) = ƒ′(g(x)) * g′(x).

Implicit Differentiation: To find dy/dx, we simply differentiate both sides of the equation y2 = x with respect to x, treating y as a differentiable function of x and applying the Chain Rule.

Implicit Differentiation Process:

  1. Differentiate both sides of the equation with respect to x
  2. Collect the terms with dy>dx on one side of the equation.
  3. Factor out dy/dx.
  4. Solve for dy/dx.

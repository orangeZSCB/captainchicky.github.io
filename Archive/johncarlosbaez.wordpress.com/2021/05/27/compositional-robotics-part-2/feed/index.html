]]> By: Martin Wen-Yu Lo https://johncarlosbaez.wordpress.com/2021/05/27/compositional-robotics-part-2/#comment-170704 Thu, 27 May 2021 17:38:13 +0000 http://johncarlosbaez.wordpress.com/?p=31082#comment-170704 I wonder if the “compositionality of robotics” will also consider the complexity of the “path planning problem”. The “Konigsberg Bridge Problem” and the “Traveling Salesmen Problem” are simple examples that path planning is a non-trivial subject. When robotic spacecraft are introduced, the dynamics of the path becomes an even more challenging problem.There is no serious treatment of the categorical nature of space trajectory design at present to my knowledge. Without a detailed knowledge of the possible space trajectories, there is no way to apply AI methods to path planning in space. Just imagine the GPS satellites without maps of the roads and cities on Earth. Knowing the detailed Lat Lon of your location will not help you get to UCLA Campus East Entrance.

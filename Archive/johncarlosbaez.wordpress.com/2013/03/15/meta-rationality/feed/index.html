]]> By: Simon K https://johncarlosbaez.wordpress.com/2013/03/15/meta-rationality/#comment-65703 Sat, 11 Apr 2015 10:17:20 +0000 http://johncarlosbaez.wordpress.com/?p=15475#comment-65703 I had independently come upon the term “meta-rational”, but with a quite different meaning than Cowen and Hanson’s. If we understand the difference between ethics and meta-ethics – ethics being theories about what actually is right or wrong, meta-ethics being theories about what it means to call something right or wrong – then by analogy, rationality would be theories about what is actually rational or irrational, and meta-rationality would be theories about what it means to call something (an argument, a belief, a person) rational or irrational. Just as meta-ethics addresses the question of what objective grounding, if any, ethical judgements have, in the same way meta-rationality must grapple with the question of what objective grounding, if any, judgements about rationality have. Another classic example of a meta-rational question is whether epistemic rationality and instrumental reality are fundamentally the same thing, or fundamentally distinct things? (see http://www.princeton.edu/~tkelly/papers/epistemicasinstrumental.pdf ) By contrast, a question like “Is Lord Monckton’s belief, that ‘there is no significant anthropogenic contribution to climate change’, rational?” is not a meta-rational question, it is a rational question.

We should expect that the imagined robotic Petri net engineers will find agreement with each other over theorems as much as mathematicians now find agreement with each other over theorems.

Next: remodeling apoptosis in terms of robotic Petri nets brings to bear a fresh mathematical tool in the attack against the diseases now associated with apoptosis run amok, like cancer.

When robotic Petri nets get involved, theorems about those robotic Petri nets (written in Haskell) get involved.

It might be a new role in the world for theorem proving.

For the game of perfect information, the player knows each other’s move, or they know what each other have done in the game. For the game of complete information, the player not necessary knows what others have done in his or her move. They don’t have information; however, they know what’s their pay off. The difference which is that, the prefect of the game is all about the knowledge of other player’s movement, or their strategy. And the game of complete information is something about the benefit what each player can gain at the end of the game.

That’s an interesting question. You can go either direction on this; just explain your reasoning in your answer. But in a typical team sport, the official rules tell us how to compute payoffs (numbers of points) for each team, and they say which team wins. They don’t give a payoff to any individual on that team.

Of course, people who follow team sports like to make up their own ways of assigning a payoff to an individual: for example, how many home runs a baseball player scores, or their batting average, etc. We can use these numbers to decide which individual wins some sort of prize. But this isn’t usually built into the rules of the game.

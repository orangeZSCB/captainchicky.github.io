On seeing the text in the picture
my first move was to smile
then move closer by zooming in.

Payoff:
The text drops away from focus and the face enters focus.

Previously, the writer of the text had been off-focus
when we focused on the text of the joke. Just an icon,
he was then just a “type” of thing as we focused on the joke.
In this situation of off-focus, he could not be identified
as being a unique human being. He could be identified only
as a type of human being.

But after the text is read, the reader’s focus
then turns to the writer of the text.

This is a cool dude with a warm joke about the dark side
of the Ultimatum Game. Or as some might call it,
trickle-down theory.

His payoff from my moving up to this level of the game–>

I want to hear his story.

—>

His move was to change his role in the game.

And by this,
he changed the level of reality
in the playing board.

What’s a joke?

A game run amuck.

–>

You are excited to play, and you feel this inside.

So the game is afoot.

He is a human being and I am a human being.

In terms of situation theory,
in the game situation his move was to metamorphose
from being a “type”–
a thing of which there may be instances–
into becoming a totally unique “instance.”

But,
an instance, in this case, of no type at all–

A totally unique human being.

Game over.

Humanity wins.

—->

Professor Baez,

With climate disasters on-rushing, seems to me we need more games like this one–

Economic games where the winner is not the utility maximizer
but humanity.

What’s interesting is that when we combine both these graphs, we can deduce that on average the sea ice in the Arctic is 1.5 meters thick (4.3 million km3/2.9 million km2). This correlates with the snippets of information we get from a couple of buoys in the Beaufort Sea (a thickness of approximately 80 cm) and measurements that was done by the scientists aboard the icebreaker Polarstern (90 cm on average around the North Pole).

We could be tempted to conclude that the PIOMAS model overestimates total ice volume, but we have to keep in mind that the multiyear ice is much thicker and makes the average higher.

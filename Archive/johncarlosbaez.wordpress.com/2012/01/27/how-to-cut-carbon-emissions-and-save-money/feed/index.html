]]> By: David Kline https://johncarlosbaez.wordpress.com/2012/01/27/how-to-cut-carbon-emissions-and-save-money/#comment-12883 Fri, 27 Jan 2012 17:19:33 +0000 http://johncarlosbaez.wordpress.com/?p=7657#comment-12883 There are lots of critiques of so-called “marginal abatement curves” like McKinsey’s. One can be found here:

http://www.tandfonline.com/doi/abs/10.1080/14693062.2011.582347

The last point in the following extract from their abstract is for me the most telling: the lack of transparency.

Legal commitments to reduce CO2 emissions require policy makers to find cost-efficient means to meet these obligations. Marginal abatement cost (MAC) curves, which illustrate the economics associated with climate change mitigation, have recently attracted a great amount of attention. A number of limitations with MAC curves are explained by the implication they should be just one tool in a broader set of decision-making aids used in assessing climate policy. MAC curves, for example, omit ancillary benefits of greenhouse gas emission abatement, treat uncertainty in a limited manner, exclude intertemporal dynamics and lack the necessary transparency concerning their assumptions.

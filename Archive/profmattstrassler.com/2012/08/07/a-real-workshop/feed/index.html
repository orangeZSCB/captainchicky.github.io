]]> By: Matt Strassler https://profmattstrassler.com/2012/08/07/a-real-workshop/#comment-16765 Mon, 13 Aug 2012 21:49:44 +0000 http://profmattstrassler.com/?p=4147#comment-16765 In reply to Younghun Park.

In principle it is possible, as far as the data is concerned. And if in fact its spin is not zero, then no, it cannot be explained by the Standard Model.

However, I would not get your hopes up. This particle behaves, so far, roughly like the spin-zero Higgs expected in the Standard Model and some of its variants. It would be quite a surprise for a spin-two particle to behave this way; it would be purely accidental for it to resemble our expectation for the Higgs (in terms of how often it is produced and how it decays.)

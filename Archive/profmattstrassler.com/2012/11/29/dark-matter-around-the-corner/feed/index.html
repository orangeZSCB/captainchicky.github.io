I’ve been corresponding with one of the authors of that paper, and I’m very excited. There’s probably enough spectral resolution from NuSTAR to exclude redshifted iron lines (6.4 keV) for anything too faint to be in SIMBAD as a nearish galaxy, so there is a very good chance of not only getting a reasonable statistical census of Milky Way sources, but also of being able to characterize the 20-60 keV envelope which is the only thing likely to distinguish stellar mass black holes from larger ones accreting only from the low density interstellar medium, all from the data they’ve already collected.

The NuSTAR team was corresponding with me frequently and promptly, about every month up until I asked them why they weren’t doing such a survey themselves when it became apparent that they weren’t just before AAS last week, after which I have not received a single reply.

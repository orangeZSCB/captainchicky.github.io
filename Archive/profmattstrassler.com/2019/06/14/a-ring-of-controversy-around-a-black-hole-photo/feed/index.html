The connection between the plane of the accretion disk and the rotation plane of the black hole is not obvious at all. I’m not able to give a informed answer yet about what is and isn’t known, but I can give you my present understanding. Simulations do not show that the planes must be the same. If the black hole is huge and ancient and the disk new, they could be uncorrelated; if, on the other hand, the disk is old and has been fueling the black hole for a long time, then its rotation may now be imprinted on the black hole. Also, there are some effects that can tend to orient the disk along the black hole’s equator, but these effects do not necessarily act rapidly. One of these was in the news recently, though I can’t attest to the accuracy or clarity of the article: https://www.universetoday.com/142453/black-hole-simulation-solves-a-mystery-about-their-accretion-disks/ .

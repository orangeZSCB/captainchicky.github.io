Well, that’s a little ambiguously stated. The four-momentum of the Z would only be known if the electron-positron pair were measured. But if they aren’t measured, then the four-momentum isn’t known.

But if where you are headed is to see if the velocity of the Z is faster than light, you can stop that line of thinking with this observation: when virtual photons are exchanged between two electrons that bounce elastically off each other, the velocity of those virtual photons is — if you calculate it the way you’re trying to do it — infinite. (Momentum is exchanged; energy is not; so v = p/E = infinite.) Of course that’s the point: virtual photons aren’t particles, and if you start to think about them that way you will get into trouble. See my post on virtual particles: https://profmattstrassler.com/articles-and-posts/particle-physics-basics/virtual-particles-what-are-they/

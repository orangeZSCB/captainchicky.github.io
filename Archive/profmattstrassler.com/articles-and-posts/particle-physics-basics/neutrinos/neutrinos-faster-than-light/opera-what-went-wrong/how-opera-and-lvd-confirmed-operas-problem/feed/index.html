Let’s say the there’s an air gap of 1 millimeter in the suspect connector. The speed of light in air is only a little less than in vacuum, so still around 3 times 10 to the 8th meters per second (rounded up). So, an extra 1 millimeter distance to travel for a light pulse would only take about 1/10th of a nanosecond, unless I did my math wrong. To account for the 60 nanosecond delay the fiber optic connector would have to be loose by about 6 meters. So there must be some other mechanism involved in this reported signal delay, other than simply extra distance traveled by the light pulse/signal.

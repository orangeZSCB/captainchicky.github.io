assuming that inside a nucleus there are anti-neutrons instead of neutrons and considerung a nucleus in the context of my ‘hypotron theory’ mentioned above
the hypotron configuration of Na-24 is : (107,104,52,37) , i.e. two prime numbers 107 and 37 do occur
and
the hypotron configuration of Na-25 is : (111,112,56,39) , i.e. NO prime numbers occur

this means that N-24 is qualitatively different from and N-25
with respect to the hypotron configuration of the nucleus,
i.e. the total numbers of low-plus-hypotrons and low-minus-hypotrons and high-plus-hypotrons and high-minus-hypotrons

if one assumes that there is some intrinsic dynamics of the nucleus,
then particular dynamical features might be depend (somehow) on the hypotron configuration of the nucleus,
i.e. if prime numbers are present then the nucleus has a large half-life

in the same way one can argue in the context of the quark-model ( considering neutrons inside nucleus ) :
Na-24 corresponds to 35 up-quarks + 37 down-quarks ( 37 is prime )
Na-25 corresponds to 36 up-quarks + 39 down-quarks ( no prime numbers )
i.e. half-life of Na-24 can be expected to be greater than half-life of Na-25

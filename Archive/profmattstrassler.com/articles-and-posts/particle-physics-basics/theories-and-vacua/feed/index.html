Yes. For example, light behaves as a wave; single photons can pass through two slits (As in the famed Double Slit Experiment) yet light waves show no evidence of ‘currents’ in some medium such as might be experienced by waves on the ocean. The best way we know of to explain this currently is to have light waves be excitations in a quantum field. (As opposed to a ‘classical’ medium like ‘aether’ which we have searched for and not found.)

The equations can get a bit finicky, but it’s possible to write down a handful of them which explain WHY the universe does a lot of the things it does. (Such as all light traveling at the exact same speed despite having different energy content.) It’s astounding what waves and fields can do. For example, the uncertainty principle becomes wonderfully obvious once you understand everything as waves, as opposed to just having to accept it as something that ‘just is’.

I am not a professor but I think I can answer that question. Yes, the electron will annihilate with the positron and not with the proton. This is because positrons and protons are positively charged and like charges repel. However, the resulting gamma rays don’t necessarily collide with the proton unless it was very, very lucky (or unlucky, depending on how you like to view it). So yes, “on occasion” the proton will be affected by one of the two gamma rays but because the nucleus is tens of thousands of times smaller than the atom itself, so it is very unlikely to happen.

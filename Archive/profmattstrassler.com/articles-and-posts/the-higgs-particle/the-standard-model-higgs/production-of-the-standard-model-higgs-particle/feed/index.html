]]> By: Matt Strassler https://profmattstrassler.com/articles-and-posts/the-higgs-particle/the-standard-model-higgs/production-of-the-standard-model-higgs-particle/#comment-6732 Sat, 25 Feb 2012 22:39:26 +0000 http://profmattstrassler.com/?page_id=2002#comment-6732 In reply to Thilo.

Production of the Higgs in an electron-positron collider occurs via:

electron + positron –> virtual Z particle –> Z + Higgs

For this to happen you need

electron energy + positron energy > (Z mass + Higgs mass) times c^2.

This process allowed the LEP collider to rule out the Standard Model Higgs particle up to 115 GeV/c-squared.

In principle you can have the process

electron+positron –> Higgs

But the rate for this process is extremely tiny, unobservably so.

Both processes would occur in a muon collider, but

muon + anti-muon –> Higgs

is larger than at an electron-positron collider by (m_muon/ m_electron)^2 ~ 400,000. That makes it large enough to observe. By setting the muon collider so that the energy per collision is exactly the Higgs mass, you can produce many more Higgs particles than you could by setting the energy higher and studying muo + antimuon –> Z + Higgs. This is what makes a muon collider potentially exciting.

But it is still many years off.

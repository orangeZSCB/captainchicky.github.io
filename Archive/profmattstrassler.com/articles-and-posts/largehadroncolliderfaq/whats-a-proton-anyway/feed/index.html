The energy is intrinsic to the proton, and comes mainly, as Matt explains, from the motion energy of the quarks and the binding energy of the strong force between quarks carried by gluons. It can’t run down because there’s nowhere to run: it is already at its lowest possible level for this type of bound system. The only way the energy can change is if there is more energy forced in from outside the system, e.g. from intense gravitational pressure inside a star, or by smashing another proton into it in the LHC.

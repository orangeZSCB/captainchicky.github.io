You don’t want to use the term “density of gravity” here, but you do mean, I think, the strength of the gravitational field in your vicinity. And it isn’t just a perception of time; how time itself evolves is dependent on the gravitational field. Time does not flow uniformly. It flows differently at the earth’s surface than it does 1000’s of miles up, where the gravitational field of the earth is weaker, and the GPS system has to correct for this effect in order for it to work. (Every time you use a GPS device to get somewhere you test Einstein’s theory!) However… that effect is taken into account in the OPERA experiment.

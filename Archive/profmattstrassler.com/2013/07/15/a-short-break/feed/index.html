The Higgs produces an attractive force between massive particles proportional to their masses. Because the Higgs is extremely massive, the force is short ranged like that of the weak bosons. Because stable particles (electrons, up and down quarks) have relatively weak interactions with the Higgs, the Higgs force is much, much weaker than the weak force. But, it’s there.

In this article we speak of mass loss but in fact, we should write “rest mass” loss. The Einstein equivalence equation between matter and energy is true only for an object that is not moving. For an object that is moving, the total energy is greater than mass times the square of the speed of light. An object’s energy can change when its motion changes, but its mass never changes. This notion of mass is sometimes called “rest mass” because it is related to the energy stored in the object when it is not moving or “at rest”. There is another interpretation of mass called “relativistic mass” but it is not commonly used among nuclear/particle physicists. (Source: profmattstrassler.com) […]
